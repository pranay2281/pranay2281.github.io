Introduction

With current rates of inflation being ~7.5%, the purchasing power of the US $ is decreasing day by day. The solution to this is investing in the stock market.

The world works on stock market. Its relevance in the economic world is of the highest stature. Stock market has always been my subject of interest. Companies and countries build and break depending on how the stock market performs. My dad being a stockbroker ensured I was well informed and aware about how the stock market works. Both of us frequently discuss different stocks and its probable future. The market has been active for over a hundred years and it is often called a ‘net worth multiplier’.

Compound interest is often called the 8th wonder of the world and investing works on that principle. Many people still fear to invest in the market. So I want to take my project on those lines. It will be to convince people to invest.

Explain Volatility

Volatility is a key concept to understand before we decide to where to invest. In the last decade, cryptocurrency has been on rise. However it might not be considered as the best investment considering how volatile it is. The diagram on the right considers the leaders in stocks (Apple Inc.) and crypto (Bitcoin). The Box Plot shows the changes in Price from October 2021 to April 2022. One can clearly see how the difference in the quartiles for Bitcoin and Apple. It is easier to predict an Apple Stock than the Bitcoin Price. Bitcoin has multiple outliers on both sides whereas Apple only has a single outlier. One might say thst Bitcoin has more positive outliers but its still an outlier, you will not be able to predict bitcoin's behaviour.

Since you are "investing" in the market, you would want to play a safer bet in the long run than choose to put it in something so risky. Bitcoin (as of May 5th) is over 50% below its all time high, whereas Apple is only 20% despite the Russia - Ukraine conflict.

Why invest and not day trade?

Options and Futures have given the recent millenials a get rich quick scheme. You can lose over a 100% of your money if you don't get your shot right. Day Trading is risky, you cannot predict what can happen the next day, but you can definitely see a company's future 5 years down the line.

A lot of external factors like interest rates, global markets, exchange rates, tax rates, employee unions, lockdown etc, all these affect daily price. Long term gains are based on company values, profits, revenue and its growth. The pandemic slashed the market by half, but it has always increased. We see a similar drop in 2008 recession as well.

Market Capital

To invest in the right companies, one must understand that the market capital is more important than share price. Here we see that a random company that no one has heard of has a price of over $4000 but a company like Microsoft has a share price of $280. Even though the share price is 1/10, Microsoft is worth 500 times more than Seaboard. The lower the share price, the more chances that it is traded frequently in the market.

Which App to use?

Fidelity, Ameritrade and Charles Schwab are clearly the best rated that are available to use. Other popular millenial apps like Robinhoob and Webull are also available to use. Personally, Charles Schwab is highly rated for investment accounts. It gives you more control over your investments. It even includes IRAs so one can plan their retirement.

How to allocate your portfolio?

It is advisable to have 25% in ETFs, as it is considered a safe bet. S&P 500 is considered to be the best one. Blue Chip stocks include, Apple, Amazon, Google etc. Medical and Automobile industry are backbones of a nation and hence they are bound to increase in the long run. Oil atleast in the forseeable future is a lucrative investment. Always have 10% cash on the table for sudden expenses or you see an unexpected opportunity to invest in the market.
